The Many Deaths of Laila Starr

Author: Ram V.

Illustrator: Felipe Andrade

Publisher: BOOM! Studios

Published: 2021

Genre: Graphic Novel / Mythology / Fantasy

When humanity discovers the secret to immortality, Death herself is made redundant. Cast out of the divine and forced to live as a mortal woman named Laila Starr, she must navigate the confusion, beauty, and pain of human existence. Through a series of encounters across different lifetimes, Laila comes to understand something she never could as an immortal: it is the ending that gives everything its meaning.

Meet the Creators

Ram V.

Writer

Ram V is an award-winning author and creator of comics and graphic novels such as Grafity's Wall, These Savage Shores, and Blue in Green. Since publishing his first book in 2016, Ram's work has gone on to garner critical and popular success and multiple awards. Apart from creating original work, since 2018, Ram has also written for iconic characters and titles at DC Comics and Marvel Entertainment.

Key Works

  • Swamp Thing
  • Batman: Reptilian
  • These Savage Shores
  • Grafity's Wall

Felipe Andrade

Illustrator

Felipe Andrade is a Brazilian comic artist celebrated for his expressive linework and emotionally rich color palettes. His style blends realism with dreamlike abstraction, making him a perfect collaborator for this mythological story.

Key Works

Story Timeline

The journey of Death learning to be human

Chapter 1

Death Loses Her Job

Laila is stripped of her divine role after humanity discovers immortality and is cast down to Earth as a mortal.

"What happens when death is no longer needed?"

"You are no longer necessary."

Chapter 2

The First Death

Laila experiences human vulnerability for the first time and encounters Darius, the boy whose birth triggered immortality.

"To be mortal is to be afraid."

"I did not know that breathing could hurt."

Chapter 3

Learning to Live

Laila struggles with hunger, loneliness, and desire. She begins to understand why humans fear death so deeply.

"You cannot understand life until you have something to lose."

"She was beginning to understand the weight of being alive."

Chapter 4

The Meaning of Endings

Laila witnesses love, loss, and the passage of time. She starts to see death not as an end but as what makes moments precious.

"Every goodbye makes every hello matter more."

"Nothing is beautiful because it lasts. Everything is beautiful because it does not."

Chapter 5

The Final Encounter

Laila meets Darius one last time as an old man. She finally understands her true purpose.

"Death is not the enemy of life. It is its guardian."

"I think I finally understand what I was for."

Ending

The Cycle Continues

Laila accepts her fate and returns to her role, transformed by her human experience.

"We are not valuable because we live forever. We are valuable because we do not."

"And so it goes. And so it must."
